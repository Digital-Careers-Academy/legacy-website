What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help websites function properly, remember preferences, and improve performance. Some cookies are set by the website you visit (“first-party cookies”), and others may be set by third-party services (“third-party cookies”).

How we use cookies

Human Legacy uses cookies to:

  • Keep the platform secure (e.g., preventing abuse and enabling safe sessions).
  • Ensure core functionality (e.g., form flows, navigation reliability).
  • Improve performance and UX (e.g., understanding usage patterns if analytics is enabled).

Types of cookies

Essential cookies (always on)

These cookies are necessary for the website to function and cannot be switched off in our systems. They are typically set in response to actions you take, such as logging in or filling forms securely.

Required
Analytics cookies (optional)

These cookies help us understand how visitors use the site so we can improve performance and usability. We only set analytics cookies if you opt in.

Optional

Managing your preferences

You can manage cookie preferences at any time. If you previously accepted optional cookies, you can change your choice by opening the cookie settings panel and saving your updated preferences.

Contact support

You can also adjust cookie settings in your browser. Note that blocking essential cookies may impact functionality.

Retention

Cookie duration can be:

  • Session cookies: removed when you close your browser.
  • Persistent cookies: remain until they expire or you delete them.

We store your cookie preference so we don’t repeatedly prompt you. You may reset consent at any time.
